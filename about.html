Preserving the Past

We are more than a shop; we are the guardians of architectural heritage and craftsmanship.

Our Sourcing Philosophy

Heritage Salvage was born from a passion for objects that carry the weight of history. We believe that the materials used in the past—old-growth timber, hand-forged iron, and artisanal glass—possess a soul that modern manufacturing simply cannot replicate.

Our team travels across Europe and North America, working with demolition specialists and estate managers to identify and rescue architectural elements that would otherwise be lost to time.

Our Conservation Ethics

Minimal Intervention

We believe in preserving the 'as-found' condition as much as possible, only intervening where structural stability or safety is compromised.

Period Authenticity

Every replacement material—from screws to stains—must be period-correct to maintain the historical integrity of the piece.

Material Longevity

We use time-tested natural materials like beeswax, shellac, and linseed oil that have protected these treasures for centuries.

A Century of Salvage

Our journey from a local workshop to a global archive of architectural history.

1998

Founded as a small restoration workshop in East London, specializing in Victorian sash window repair.

2005

Expanded into large-scale architectural salvage, securing first major estate acquisition in the Cotswolds.

2012

Established a global sourcing network with dedicated teams across France, Italy, and the UK.

2024

Opened our flagship digital archive and modern conservation facility, blending heritage with modern logistics.
